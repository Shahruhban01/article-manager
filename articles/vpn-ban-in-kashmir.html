Respectfully, the issue is not whether tools like VPNs are legal or illegal.

The real issue is how technology-related laws are perceived and enforced on the ground in Kashmir, often without technical understanding or contextual judgment.

In many cases, the word "VPN" alone triggers suspicion. The presence of such tools on a phone or laptop is treated as a red flag even before understanding the purpose behind their use.

I am a college student and an application developer. For learning and development, I connect my devices to my own cloud-hosted server on platforms like AWS using private networking tools such as Tailscale.

This is not a commercial VPN, not for hiding identity, and not for bypassing the law. It is a technical necessity for testing, debugging, and learning modern cloud systems.

Due to financial limitations, I do not own a high-end computer. Cloud free-tier services allow students like me to learn real-world skills despite hardware constraints.

Recently, I approached my local police station seeking clarity. I was informed that no permission could be granted and that approval would be required from higher administrative offices.

Should a student spend time building skills, or running between offices explaining basic technology?

Over time, repeated fear and uncertainty create an unintended consequence. When students observe how clearly and practically technology laws are applied in other countries, it naturally creates a desire to pursue education and careers elsewhere.

This is not due to lack of attachment to one's homeland, but a search for clarity, predictability, and freedom to learn.

I ask not for exemptions, but for understanding, awareness, and fairness. Fear does not create security. Clarity creates confidence.

Frequently Asked Questions

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) is a technology that creates a secure and encrypted connection between devices. VPNs can be used for privacy, security, or connecting to private systems. Like any technology tool, misuse should be punished, not legitimate academic or professional use.

Tailscale is a private networking tool widely used by developers and organizations to securely connect their own devices and servers. It is commonly used for application development, testing, debugging, and learning modern cloud-based systems.
